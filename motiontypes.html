Types of Motion

  1. Periodic Motion

    A motion which repeats itself identically after a fixed interval of time is called periodic motion.
    e.g., orbital motion of the earth around the sun, motion of arms of a clock, motion of a simple pendulum etc

  2. Oscillatory Motion

    Oscillatory motion is defined as the to and fro motion of the body about its fixed position. Oscillatory motion is a type of periodic motion.
    Ex - vibrating strings, swinging of the swing etc.

  3. Circulatory Motion

    When an object moves on a circular path with constant or uniform speed, it is said to have uniform circular motion.
    Ex - motion of hands of a clock, motion of moon revolving around the earth

  4. Rectilinear Motion

    If the motion of a body is along a straight line, it is said to be the rectilinear or linear motion.
    Ex - the motion of an arrow from a bow.

  5. Curvilinear Motion

    The motion of a particle or object moving along a curved path is called curvilinear motion.
    Ex - Throwing paper airplanes, motion of a snake, motion of a basketball into the basket

  6. Translatory Motion

    The motion in which all the particles of a body move through the same distance in the same time is called translatory motion. There are two types of translatory motions: rectilinear motion; curvilinear motion. Ex - of translatory motion are: A running boy, moving bus, walking cat running train
